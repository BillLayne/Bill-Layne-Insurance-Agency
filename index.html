An SR-22 in North Carolina is a certificate of financial responsibility filed with the NC DMV proving you carry at least the state minimum auto insurance. It is typically required after a DWI, driving without insurance, or certain moving violations. We specialize in SR-22 filings and can often get you covered and filed the same day. Call (336) 835-1993.
A captive agent (like State Farm or Allstate agents) can only sell policies from one company. An independent agent like Bill Layne Insurance represents multiple carriers such as Progressive, Travelers, and Nationwide, allowing us to compare rates and coverage options across companies to find the best fit for your needs and budget. You get more choices and we work for you, not the insurance company.
While not legally required in NC, renters insurance is highly recommended. Your landlord's policy does NOT cover your personal belongings if there's a fire, theft, or water damage. Renters insurance also provides liability coverage if someone is injured in your apartment. Policies typically start around $15-$25 per month and can be bundled with your auto insurance for potential additional savings.
We recommend reviewing your insurance at least once a year or whenever you experience a major life change such as buying a new home, adding a teen driver, getting married, or starting a business. In North Carolina, driving without insurance is a Class 1 misdemeanor. If your coverage lapses, the NC DMV can revoke your registration and license plates. You may also face higher premiums when you reinstate coverage because carriers view a lapse as a risk factor. If your policy has lapsed, call us immediately at (336) 835-1993 so we can get you re-covered quickly.
Yes, most insurance carriers in NC use a credit-based insurance score as one factor in determining your premium. This is different from your lending credit score. Factors like payment history and outstanding debt can influence your rate. Because each carrier weighs credit differently, shopping multiple carriers through an independent agent like us can help you find a better rate regardless of your credit situation.
The Safe Driver Incentive Plan (SDIP) is North Carolina's unique points system that directly affects your insurance premiums. Points are assigned for at-fault accidents and moving violations and remain on your record for 3 years. Even one speeding ticket can increase your rate. We can review your SDIP record and shop carriers that offer the most competitive rates for your point level. Call (336) 835-1993 for a free review.
