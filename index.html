Host the Ultimate Football Party, Worry-Free!

The NFL season is finally here! Before your guests arrive, make sure your North Carolina home is protected with these essential insurance tips.

Published: September 3, 2025

The smell of barbecue is in the air, jerseys are on, and the roar of the crowd is about to fill your living room. Football season kickoff is a North Carolina tradition, but a packed house for the big game can bring unexpected risks. A guest tripping over a rug or an accidental grill fire can turn a touchdown celebration into a major financial fumble. The good news? A little preparation and a solid understanding of your homeowners insurance can be your best defense.

Your Insurance Playbook: What's Covered?

Think of your standard NC homeowners policy as your starting lineup. It already has key players on the field ready to protect you during your NFL party.

Personal Liability (Coverage E)

This is your star player. If a guest is injured at your party—say, they slip on a spilled drink—and you're found legally responsible, this coverage helps pay for their medical bills and your legal defense fees, up to your policy limits.

Medical Payments (Coverage F)

This coverage is for smaller incidents. It helps pay for minor medical expenses for a guest injured on your property, regardless of who is at fault. It's a great way to handle small accidents quickly and maintain goodwill.

Potential Red Zones: Gaps in Your Coverage

While your standard policy is strong, certain party-related situations can be tricky. The biggest one? Alcohol.

The Challenge of "Social Host Liability"

In North Carolina, if you serve alcohol to a guest who then leaves your party and causes an accident, you could be held liable. This is known as "Social Host Liability." Many standard insurance policies have limitations or exclusions for alcohol-related incidents. It's crucial to check your specific policy language. If you're concerned, this is where a special teams player can save the day.

Pro Tip: Consider an Umbrella Policy. This adds an extra layer of liability protection (often $1 million or more) on top of your existing homeowners policy, offering crucial coverage for major claims.

Pre-Game Checklist for a Winning Party

The best way to avoid a claim is to prevent accidents from happening in the first place. Run through this checklist before your guests arrive:

  • Clear the Field: Ensure walkways, stairs, and high-traffic areas are clear of clutter. Tape down any loose rugs or cables.
  • Grill Safety: Keep your grill a safe distance from your house, deck railings, and any overhanging branches. Have a fire extinguisher nearby and never leave it unattended.
  • Food Safety: Keep hot foods hot and cold foods cold to prevent foodborne illness. Be mindful of any guest allergies.
  • Responsible Hosting: Serve plenty of food and non-alcoholic beverages. Stop serving alcohol well before the party ends. Arrange for a designated driver or rideshare for anyone who has had too much to drink.
  • Secure Your Valuables: Put away fragile items and lock up valuables in a separate room to prevent accidental damage or theft.
