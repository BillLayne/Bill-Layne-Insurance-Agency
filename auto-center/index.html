While NC requires 50/100/50, we recommend 100/300/100 to properly protect your assets and future earnings.
Driving record, age, vehicle type, credit score, and location all play a role. NC uses the "Safe Driver Incentive Plan" (SDIP) which adds surcharges for violations. As of July 2025, serious violations (4+ points) now carry a 5-year surcharge period, and new drivers face an 8-year inexperienced operator surcharge.
You may be able to reduce your premium by bundling home and auto policies, maintaining a clean driving record, asking about available discounts, increasing your deductible if you have adequate savings, and comparing quotes from multiple carriers through an independent agent.
The NC Safe Driver Incentive Plan is a statewide system that adds surcharge points to your insurance for moving violations and at-fault accidents. As of July 1, 2025, violations carrying 4 or more points now stay on your record for 5 years (up from 3). Minor violations under 4 points still follow a 3-year surcharge period. Prayer for Judgment (PJC) is now limited to 1 per policy every 5 years. Drivers first licensed after July 1, 2025 face an inexperienced operator surcharge for 8 years instead of 3.
An independent agent like Bill Layne Insurance represents multiple insurance carriers, comparing quotes from several companies on your behalf. A captive agent works for only one company and can only offer that company's products. Independent agents can help you find more competitive pricing by shopping your coverage across multiple carriers.
Yes, uninsured motorist (UM) coverage is strongly recommended in NC. While not legally required, NC has one of the higher rates of uninsured drivers in the country. UM coverage protects you if you're hit by a driver who has no insurance or not enough insurance to cover your damages. Most insurance professionals recommend carrying UM limits equal to your liability limits.
Letting your auto insurance lapse in North Carolina can have serious consequences. The NC DMV may suspend your registration and license plates. You'll face a $50 reinstatement fee per vehicle and may need to file an FR-44 or SR-22 certificate proving financial responsibility. Insurance companies also charge higher premiums to drivers with coverage gaps. If caught driving without insurance, you face fines up to $200 and possible vehicle impoundment.
An independent agent like Bill Layne Insurance can access rates from multiple carriers simultaneously — something you can't do with a captive agent or by calling one company at a time. Because carriers price risk differently, the same driver can see rates vary by hundreds of dollars between companies. We do the comparison shopping for you and help identify discounts you may qualify for, all at no extra cost to you.
