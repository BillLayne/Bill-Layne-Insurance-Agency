🚨 Accident Scene Checklist

The first few minutes after an accident are crucial for your safety and your insurance claim. Here's exactly what to do.

Immediate Safety Steps (First 5 Minutes)

1
Check for injuries
Your safety and others' safety comes first. Call 911 if anyone is hurt.
2
Move to safety
If possible, move vehicles out of traffic. Turn on hazard lights.
3
Call 911
Even for minor accidents. Police report helps with claims.
4
Turn off engines
Reduce fire risk and further damage.

Information to Collect

👥 From Other Driver(s)
  • • Full name and contact information
  • • Driver's license number
  • • Insurance company and policy number
  • • Vehicle make, model, year, color
  • • License plate number
  • • Relationship to vehicle owner (if different)
👮 From Police/Scene
  • • Police report number
  • • Badge numbers of responding officers
  • • Witness names and contact info
  • • Exact location of accident
  • • Time and date
  • • Weather and road conditions

Document Everything

📱 Use Your Phone Strategically

Take these photos immediately (before vehicles are moved if safe):

  • Overall scene: Wide shots showing all vehicles and road conditions
  • Vehicle damage: Close-ups of all damage from multiple angles
  • License plates: All vehicles involved
  • Driver's licenses: Other drivers' licenses (with permission)
  • Insurance cards: Other drivers' insurance information
  • Street signs/landmarks: To establish exact location
  • Skid marks/debris: Evidence of impact and vehicle paths

❌ DON'T Do These Things

  • Don't admit fault - Even saying "I'm sorry" can be used against you
  • Don't speculate - Stick to facts about what you observed
  • Don't sign anything - Except police citations if required
  • Don't accept cash - Always go through insurance
  • Don't leave the scene - Even if damage seems minor
  • Don't argue - Stay calm and factual

📞 Reporting Your Claim

Report your claim as soon as possible after the accident. Many insurers have 24/7 claim reporting.

When to Report

Immediately
Major Accidents
Injuries, multiple vehicles, significant damage, or if police were called.
Within 24 Hours
All Other Accidents
Even minor fender-benders. Some damage isn't visible immediately.
Before Leaving Scene
Hit-and-Run
Report immediately - evidence disappears quickly.

How to Report Your Claim

1
📞 Call Your Agent

Start with Bill Layne Insurance: (336) 835-1993. We'll guide you through the process and can three-way call your insurance company.

2
📱 Use Insurer's App

Most major insurers have mobile apps for claim reporting. You can upload photos and track progress.

3
🌐 File Online

Visit your insurance company's website to start a claim online if you prefer not to call.

Information You'll Need to Provide

📋 Claim Report Checklist

  • Policy information: Your policy number and coverage details
  • Accident details: Date, time, location, weather conditions
  • Other parties: Names, contact info, insurance details
  • Vehicle information: Make, model, year, VIN, license plates
  • Damage description: What you can see that's damaged
  • Witness information: Names and contact details
  • Police report: Report number and responding agency
  • Photos: Upload or email pictures from the scene

💡 Pro Tip

Ask for your claim number immediately and write it down. You'll need this for all future communications. Also ask for your adjuster's name and direct contact information.

🔍 Investigation Process

Once you file a claim, your insurance company begins investigating to determine fault, coverage, and settlement amounts.

What the Insurance Company Investigates

🕵️ Fault Determination
  • • Police report analysis
  • • Witness statements
  • • Vehicle damage patterns
  • • Traffic laws and violations
  • • Road conditions and visibility
  • • Driver statements
💰 Damage Assessment
  • • Vehicle inspection by adjuster
  • • Repair estimates from body shops
  • • Market value for total losses
  • • Medical records for injuries
  • • Lost wages documentation
  • • Other economic losses

Types of Adjusters You Might Work With

🏢

Staff Adjuster

Employee of your insurance company. Usually handles routine claims and has authority to settle within certain limits.

🤝

Independent Adjuster

Third-party contractor hired by insurance company. Often used for complex claims or when staff adjusters are overloaded.

👩‍⚖️

Public Adjuster

Works for you (the policyholder), not the insurance company. Charges a fee but may get higher settlements for complex claims.

Investigation Timeline

Day 1-3
Initial Contact
Adjuster assigned, initial phone interview, claim number issued.
Day 3-7
Vehicle Inspection
Adjuster inspects damage, takes photos, gets repair estimates.
Week 1-2
Fault Investigation
Police report reviewed, witnesses contacted, liability determined.
Week 2-4
Settlement Offer
Initial settlement offer made based on investigation findings.

🚨 Investigation Red Flags

  • Long delays without communication or explanation
  • Adjuster unavailable - not returning calls or emails
  • Requesting excessive documentation for simple claims
  • Blaming you without clear evidence
  • Lowball damage estimates significantly below repair shop quotes
  • Rushing your decisions on settlement offers

💰 Types of Settlements

Understanding different settlement types helps you know what to expect and ensure you get fair compensation.

Vehicle Damage Settlements

🔧

Repair Settlement

Insurance pays to repair your vehicle minus your deductible.

Best for: Newer vehicles with manageable damage

💸

Total Loss Settlement

Vehicle declared total loss - you get Actual Cash Value minus deductible.

Best for: Older vehicles or when repair costs exceed value

💵

Cash Settlement

You receive money and keep the damaged vehicle.

Best for: When you want to handle repairs yourself

How Total Loss Value is Calculated

📊 Actual Cash Value (ACV) Formula

ACV = Replacement Cost - Depreciation

Factors that Increase Value:

  • • Low mileage
  • • Recent maintenance
  • • Excellent condition
  • • Popular model/features
  • • Original equipment

Factors that Decrease Value:

  • • High mileage
  • • Poor maintenance
  • • Previous accidents
  • • Wear and tear
  • • Aftermarket modifications

Injury-Related Settlements

🏥 What's Typically Covered

  • Medical expenses: Emergency room, hospital, doctor visits, physical therapy
  • Lost wages: Time off work due to injuries
  • Pain and suffering: Non-economic damages (varies by state and severity)
  • Future medical costs: Ongoing treatment needs
  • Disability compensation: If injuries cause permanent disability
  • Loss of consortium: Impact on relationships (spouse/family)

💡 Settlement Negotiation Tips

  • Don't accept the first offer - Initial offers are often low
  • Get multiple repair estimates - Use certified shops
  • Research your vehicle's value - Use KBB, Edmunds, AutoTrader
  • Document everything - Keep receipts, photos, medical records
  • Understand your policy limits - Know maximum payouts
  • Consider all damages - Don't forget rental car, towing, etc.

Factors That Affect Timeline

⚡ Claims That Settle Quickly

  • • Clear fault determination
  • • Minor damage, no injuries
  • • Good documentation
  • • Cooperative parties
  • • Straightforward repairs
  • • No disputes over value

🐌 Claims That Take Longer

  • • Disputed fault
  • • Serious injuries
  • • Multiple parties involved
  • • Complex damage assessment
  • • Uncooperative parties
  • • Legal action required

What You Can Do to Speed Up Your Claim

🚀 Acceleration Tips

  • Respond quickly to adjuster requests for information
  • Provide complete documentation upfront
  • Be available for vehicle inspections and interviews
  • Get multiple repair estimates from certified shops
  • Follow up regularly but don't be a pest
  • Keep detailed records of all communications
  • Ask specific questions about what's needed to move forward

North Carolina Claim Requirements

⚖️ NC Insurance Laws

  • 30-day investigation: Insurers must investigate claims within 30 days
  • Settlement timeline: Must settle or deny within 30 days of agreement
  • Payment deadline: Must pay within 30 days of settlement agreement
  • Communication requirement: Must communicate claim status every 45 days
  • Interest on delays: May owe interest if payments are unreasonably delayed

🤝 Working with Adjusters

Your relationship with the adjuster can significantly impact your claim outcome. Here's how to work effectively together.

Understanding the Adjuster's Role

🎯 What Adjusters Do
  • • Investigate the facts of your claim
  • • Determine coverage under your policy
  • • Assess fault and liability
  • • Evaluate damages and calculate settlements
  • • Negotiate settlement terms
  • • Authorize claim payments

Remember: Adjusters work for the insurance company, but most try to be fair and professional.

Building a Good Working Relationship

✅ DO These Things

  • Be honest and factual - Stick to what you know
  • Be responsive - Return calls and emails promptly
  • Be organized - Have documents ready when requested
  • Ask questions - Don't hesitate to ask for clarification
  • Take notes - Document all conversations
  • Be professional - Stay calm and courteous

❌ DON'T Do These Things

  • Don't lie or exaggerate - This can void your claim
  • Don't be hostile - Anger doesn't help negotiations
  • Don't ignore requests - This can delay your claim
  • Don't accept recorded statements without understanding why
  • Don't settle too quickly - Make sure you understand all damages
  • Don't sign releases until you're satisfied

Key Questions to Ask Your Adjuster

📝 Important Questions

  • Coverage questions: "What specific coverage applies to my claim?"
  • Timeline questions: "When can I expect a decision/payment?"
  • Process questions: "What are the next steps in my claim?"
  • Documentation questions: "What else do you need from me?"
  • Settlement questions: "How was this settlement amount calculated?"
  • Appeal questions: "If I disagree, what's the appeal process?"

When the Adjuster is Unreasonable

🚨 Escalation Steps

  1. Document the problem - Keep detailed records of issues
  2. Request a supervisor - Ask to speak with the adjuster's manager
  3. File a complaint - Contact your insurance company's customer service
  4. Contact your agent - We can advocate for you with the company
  5. State insurance department - File a complaint with NC DOI
  6. Consider legal help - Consult with an attorney if necessary

Vehicle Value

Negotiation Strategies

💪 Negotiation Do's

  • Research first - Know your vehicle's value and repair costs
  • Present facts - Use documentation to support your position
  • Be patient - Don't rush major decisions
  • Consider total costs - Include all related expenses
  • Get everything in writing - Verbal agreements aren't binding
  • Know when to compromise - Reasonable settlements save time

⚠️ Negotiation Don'ts

  • Don't accept first offers - They're often intentionally low
  • Don't make threats - Stay professional and factual
  • Don't settle partial claims - Wait until all damages are known
  • Don't sign releases early - Especially for injury claims
  • Don't give up too easily - Reasonable persistence pays off
  • Don't negotiate when emotional - Wait until you're calm

Common Settlement Mistakes to Avoid

🚫 Costly Mistakes

  • Settling too quickly: Before all damage is known or medical treatment is complete
  • Not considering future costs: Ongoing medical treatment, diminished vehicle value
  • Accepting blame: Admitting fault when liability is unclear
  • Not getting multiple estimates: Only using adjuster's preferred repair shops
  • Ignoring pain: Not seeking medical attention for injuries that develop later
  • Not reading settlement agreements: Understanding what you're signing away
  • Forgetting additional expenses: Rental cars, towing, missed work

When to Consider Professional Help

🆘 Get Help When...

  • Serious injuries with ongoing medical treatment
  • Disputed fault in complex accidents
  • Total loss disputes over vehicle value
  • Bad faith practices by insurance company
  • Settlement offers seem low compared to your research
  • Claim denial you believe is unfair
  • Multiple parties involved with complex liability

⚠️ Common Problems

Knowing what problems might arise helps you recognize them early and take appropriate action.

Claim Delays

🐌 Why Claims Get Delayed

  • Incomplete information: Missing documents or unclear accident details
  • Complex investigations: Multiple parties, unclear fault, or extensive damage
  • Medical complications: Ongoing treatment or uncertain injury extent
  • Adjuster overload: Too many claims, staff shortages
  • Legal issues: Subrogation, liens, or disputed coverage
  • Fraudulent claims: Company investigating suspicious activity

Lowball Settlement Offers

💸 Why Offers May Be Low
  • • Insurance companies start low expecting negotiation
  • • Adjuster may not have complete damage information
  • • Your vehicle's value may be underestimated
  • • Some damages may not be immediately apparent
  • • Computer programs may not account for special circumstances

Solution: Research, document, and negotiate professionally

Disputed Fault

🔍 When Fault is Disputed

  • Both drivers blame each other
  • No witnesses to the accident
  • Conflicting police reports
  • Complex intersection accidents
  • Weather-related factors
  • Multiple vehicle pile-ups

📋 How to Strengthen Your Case

  • Gather additional evidence
  • Find witnesses you missed initially
  • Review traffic camera footage
  • Get expert accident reconstruction
  • Document road/weather conditions
  • Review relevant traffic laws

Claim Denials

🚫 Common Reasons for Denial

  • Policy exclusions: Damage type not covered by your policy
  • Coverage lapses: Policy not in effect at time of accident
  • Misrepresentation: False information on application or claim
  • Intentional acts: Deliberate damage or criminal activity
  • Driver not covered: Unauthorized use of vehicle
  • Late reporting: Claim filed too long after accident

How to Appeal a Denial

📝 Appeal Process Steps
  1. Request written explanation - Get specific policy language cited
  2. Review your policy - Understand the coverage and exclusions
  3. Gather additional evidence - Address the reasons for denial
  4. Submit formal appeal - In writing with supporting documentation
  5. Request supervisor review - If initial appeal is denied
  6. Contact state insurance department - File a complaint if necessary
  7. Consider legal action - Consult attorney for valid claims

⚖️ NC-Specific Laws

North Carolina has specific laws and regulations that affect how auto insurance claims are handled.

North Carolina is a "Fault" State

⚖️ What This Means

  • At-fault driver pays: The driver who caused the accident is financially responsible
  • Your insurance may pay first: Then seek reimbursement from at-fault driver's insurance
  • You can sue the at-fault driver: If their insurance doesn't cover all damages
  • Comparative negligence: If you're partially at fault, your compensation is reduced

NC Insurance Claim Deadlines

Immediately
Report to Police
Required for accidents with injuries, death, or property damage over $1,000.
ASAP
Report to Insurance
Most policies require "prompt" notice. Don't wait more than a few days.
3 Years
File Lawsuit
Statute of limitations for personal injury and property damage claims.

NC Insurance Department Protections

🛡️ Your Rights Under NC Law

  • Fair Claims Handling: Insurance companies must investigate and settle claims fairly
  • Timely Communication: Must respond to communications within reasonable time
  • Complete Investigation: Must thoroughly investigate before denying claims
  • Good Faith Settlement: Must make reasonable settlement offers based on evidence
  • Prompt Payment: Must pay valid claims within 30 days of agreement
  • Interest on Delays: May owe interest if payments are unreasonably delayed

Special NC Requirements

📋 Uninsured Motorist Coverage
  • • Required in North Carolina
  • • Must match liability limits
  • • Can only be rejected in writing
  • • Covers hit-and-run accidents
  • • Applies to all household members
🚗 Vehicle Inspection Requirements
  • • Required after certain repairs
  • • Must pass safety inspection
  • • Some salvage titles require inspection
  • • Affects insurability and rates
  • • Required for registration renewal

⚠️ NC-Specific Gotchas

  • Contributory Negligence: If you're even 1% at fault, you may not recover anything from the other driver
  • Guest Statute: Passengers have limited rights to sue drivers in some circumstances
  • No-Contact Rule: Your insurer can't contact you about settling until after the police report is filed
  • Salvage Title Rules: Specific requirements for vehicles declared total losses
