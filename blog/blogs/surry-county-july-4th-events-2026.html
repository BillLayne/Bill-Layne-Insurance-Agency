Friday, July 3: Elkin FreedomFest

The weekend kicks off right here at home. FreedomFest is Elkin's free, family-friendly Independence Day celebration, and it runs the night before the Fourth so you can enjoy it and still catch Saturday's events. Music starts in the late afternoon and builds to a fireworks finale after dark.

🎆 Elkin FreedomFest
Elkin Recreation Park · 399 NC-268 W
When
Friday, July 3 — music at 5 PM, fireworks at approximately 9 PM
Where
Elkin Recreation Park, 399 NC-268 W, Elkin
Includes
Live music, food, family activities, and a fireworks show — free admission

Bring lawn chairs and arrive early for parking. Confirm the schedule with the Town of Elkin before heading out.

Bill's Two Cents

FreedomFest is my favorite night of the year in Elkin. Get to the rec park before the music starts at 5, spread out a blanket, and you'll have a perfect view of the fireworks without fighting for a spot.

Saturday, July 4: Mount Airy's parade & fireworks

Mount Airy throws the county's biggest party, and it's an all-day affair. The morning starts with a reading of the Declaration of Independence, rolls into a parade down Main Street, and includes a charity pie-eating contest before fireworks light up the night at Veterans Memorial Park. Plan for street closures and get downtown early.

How can I celebrate safely?

Half the fun of the Fourth is doing it the easy way — let the pros handle the big booms while you handle the cooler. A few simple things keep the holiday happy for everyone, pets included.

📖 In plain English

Consumer fireworks in NC — most aerial and explosive fireworks are illegal for personal use in North Carolina; only certain ground-based and hand-held novelties are generally allowed. When in doubt, leave the big show to the professionals.

  1. Enjoy the community show

    It's free, it's safer, and it's a better display than anything in a backyard.

  2. Keep grills clear

    Set up away from the house, railings, and low branches, and never leave a hot grill unattended.

  3. Bring the pets inside

    Fireworks spook animals — a quiet room indoors keeps them calm and safe.

  4. Have a sober driver

    Holiday roads are busy. Line up your ride home before the festivities start.

The safest fireworks are the ones you watch — let the community show light up the sky while you relax.

Frequently asked questions

Where can I see fireworks in Surry County on July 4th weekend 2026?
You have two big local shows. Elkin's FreedomFest lights up Friday, July 3 at Elkin Recreation Park, with fireworks around 9 p.m. Mount Airy's fireworks follow on Saturday, July 4 at Veterans Memorial Park, also after dark. Just over the county line, Yadkinville and Davie County (Mocksville) hold their own shows on July 4 as well. Confirm exact times with each organizer before you go.
What time is the Mount Airy July 4th parade in 2026?
The Mount Airy Independence Day parade begins at 11 a.m. on Saturday, July 4, 2026, downtown. Before it, there is a reading of the Declaration of Independence at 10 a.m. in the courtyard of the Mount Airy Museum of Regional History at 301 N. Main St., and afterward a charity pie-eating contest. Arrive early for a good spot along Main Street.
When is Elkin's FreedomFest in 2026?
FreedomFest is Friday, July 3, 2026 at Elkin Recreation Park, 399 NC-268 W. Live music starts at 5 p.m. and the fireworks are scheduled for approximately 9 p.m. It is a free, family-friendly event, so bring lawn chairs and plan to arrive early for parking.
Is there a July 4th event in Pilot Mountain?
Yes. Pilot Mountain hosts a July 4th Cruise-In on Saturday, July 4, 2026 at 3 p.m. on Main Street, with classic cars, food trucks, local drinks, and live beach music from Gary Lowder & Smokin' Hot. It is a relaxed afternoon event and a great option before the evening fireworks elsewhere.
Are personal fireworks legal in North Carolina?
Most aerial and explosive consumer fireworks are illegal for personal use in North Carolina; only certain ground-based and hand-held novelties are generally allowed. Beyond the legal risk, a mishap can create a real insurance headache, so for the big show it is safest to enjoy a professional community display.
