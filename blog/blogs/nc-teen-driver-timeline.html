Phase 1: The Limited Learner's Permit (Age 15-16)

This is the starting line. Your teen can get their permit once they turn 15, provided they've completed a state-approved Driver's Education course.

  • Driver's Ed Complete: Your teen needs their Driver's Education Certificate (DEC). This is your golden ticket to the DMV.
  • DMV Visit: You'll need to pass vision and written tests. Bring all your documents!
  • CRITICAL INSURANCE STEP: Before you go to the DMV, you need a Form DL-123 from your insurance agent. This proves you have liability coverage. Calling a national company can mean waiting on hold or navigating a confusing app. We can email you a DL-123 in minutes.

Action Item: Call us at 336-835-1993 before your DMV appointment. We'll add your teen to your policy and provide the DL-123 instantly, ensuring your first trip is your only trip.

Phase 2: The Limited Provisional License (Age 16-17)

After holding the Learner's Permit for at least 12 months and logging 60 hours of driving (including 10 at night), your teen is ready for the next level. This license allows them to drive unsupervised with some restrictions.

  • Road Test: This is the big one. Your teen must pass the on-the-road driving test.
  • Insurance Stays Key: You must maintain proof of insurance. This is a great time to check for new discounts. Did your teen get good grades? That's a discount. Did they take an advanced driving course? That's another discount.

Once your teen has held their Limited Provisional License for 6 months without any moving violations or seatbelt infractions, they can graduate to a Full Provisional License. This removes the time-of-day and passenger restrictions.
