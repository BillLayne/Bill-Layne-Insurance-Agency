Is Medical Payments (MedPay) Required in North Carolina for 2026?

No, Medical Payments (MedPay) is not a legally required coverage in North Carolina for the 2026 policy year. Per the North Carolina Department of Insurance (NCDOI), the only mandatory coverages are Bodily Injury Liability, Property Damage Liability, and Uninsured Motorist coverage.

While the North Carolina General Assembly recently passed Senate Bill 452, which significantly increases the minimum liability limits to 50/100/50 starting in 2025, MedPay remains an "elective" addition. What Exactly Does North Carolina MedPay Cover?

North Carolina MedPay covers a wide spectrum of "reasonable and necessary" medical expenses, ranging from ambulance fees to dental work and even funeral expenses. It applies to the named insured, resident relatives, and any passengers occupying the insured vehicle at the time of the accident.

Unlike health insurance, which may have "in-network" restrictions or require pre-authorization, MedPay is broad. Under the standard NC Personal Auto Policy (PAP), the following are typically covered:

  • Emergency Services: Ambulance transport and ER stabilization.
  • Diagnostic Tools: X-rays, MRIs, and CT scans required after a crash.
  • Professional Fees: Visits to surgeons, chiropractors, and physical therapists.
  • Dental Care: If you lose a tooth or suffer jaw trauma in the impact.
  • Prosthetics: Necessary medical devices or limbs required due to the accident.
  • Funeral Benefits: Most NC policies include a portion of the limit for funeral costs.

One of the "hidden" benefits of NC MedPay is its portability. If you are a pedestrian or a cyclist in Elkin and are struck by a motor vehicle, your own auto policy's MedPay coverage will typically kick in to pay your medical bills. This "follows the person" feature is a cornerstone of North Carolina insurance expertise that many residents overlook. MedPay vs. Health Insurance and Bodily Injury Liability

The primary difference is that MedPay pays regardless of fault and has no deductibles or co-pays, whereas Bodily Injury (BI) requires proof of negligence and health insurance often involves out-of-pocket costs. MedPay acts as a "gap-filler" that complements your other policies.

Frequently Asked Questions About NC MedPay

Q: Is Medical Payments coverage required in North Carolina?

A: No, Medical Payments (MedPay) is not required by North Carolina law. However, while it is optional, the NCDOI and most local agents recommend it to cover health insurance deductibles and immediate medical costs following an accident.

Q: Does NC MedPay cover me if I am a pedestrian?

A: Yes, North Carolina MedPay typically covers the named insured and family members even if they are struck by a vehicle while walking or cycling. This is a primary benefit that extends beyond just driving your own car.

Q: How much MedPay should I carry in 2026?

A: We recommend a minimum of $5,000 in MedPay for most North Carolina drivers. Given that the average ER visit in the Piedmont Triad area often exceeds $3,000, a $1,000 limit is no longer sufficient to cover modern medical costs.

Q: Does MedPay cover lost wages in North Carolina?

A: No, MedPay in NC only covers "reasonable and necessary" medical and funeral expenses. If you need coverage for lost wages, you would need to look into disability insurance or seek recovery through a third-party Bodily Injury claim.

Q: Will my MedPay rates go up if I use the coverage?

A: Using MedPay for a "not-at-fault" accident generally does not cause a rate increase in North Carolina. Since it is a first-party benefit designed for your protection, it is viewed differently than an at-fault liability claim.
