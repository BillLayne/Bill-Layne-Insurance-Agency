Best Companies to Work For in NC: Forbes’ 2025 Rankings Draw Attention from Job Seekers

The latest Forbes "Best-In-State Employers" list confirms North Carolina's reputation as a hub for career growth, with 100 organizations making the cut in 2025 across diverse sectors.

By Bill Layne Insurance Team | Elkin, NC | Published September 10, 2025

North Carolina continues to be a magnet for talent, driven by a robust economy and a high quality of life. Every year, professionals eagerly await the Forbes list of "America's Best Employers by State" to identify the organizations leading the pack in employee satisfaction, benefits, and culture.

The 2025 rankings, released in August by Forbes and market research firm Statista, highlight the incredible diversity of the Tar Heel State's economy. While technology and finance remain strong, the list is heavily populated by the Education and Healthcare sectors ("Eds and Meds").

For anyone considering a career move or relocating to North Carolina, this comprehensive overview of the recognized employers provides invaluable insight.

```svg How the Rankings Work

The list is based on surveys of over 160,000 employees nationwide working for companies with 500+ staff. Participants evaluate employers based on atmosphere, development, diversity, compensation, and, crucially, their willingness to recommend their employer. 100 NC employers made the 2025 list.

The 2025 Recognized Employers in North Carolina

The organizations recognized span nearly every major industry, showcasing the state's economic strength from the mountains to the coast.

🎓 Education Powerhouses (The "Eds")

Higher education remains a cornerstone of North Carolina's employment landscape, offering stability and strong benefits. Ten of the 100 recognized employers in NC were in the education sector.

Key Highlights:

  • Duke University: Ranked #10 overall in NC, marking its seventh consecutive year on the list and recognized for its "culture of belonging."
  • UNC Charlotte: Also celebrating its seventh consecutive year, praised for comprehensive benefits and professional development.
  • Wake Forest University: Recognized on the state list and also highly ranked nationally on the Forbes 2025 list of "America's Best Employers for Women."

The UNC System:

In addition to UNC Charlotte, the following UNC System institutions also made the Top 100:

  • Appalachian State University
  • UNC Chapel Hill
  • NC State University
  • Western Carolina University
  • North Carolina A&T University

⚕️ Healthcare and Insurance Leaders (The "Meds")

Major health systems and insurers are critical employers across the state, frequently cited for their advancement opportunities and investments in employee wellness.

  • Duke University Health System: Ranked #21 overall in NC, recognized alongside the University for its strong reputation.
  • Novant Health: Frequently recognized for investments in employee well-being and tuition assistance programs.
  • Blue Cross Blue Shield of North Carolina: The state's largest health insurer continues to be a top-rated employer, also appearing on the national "Best Large Employers" list.
  • FirstHealth of the Carolinas: Also recognized on the national "Best Large Employers" list.
  • Advocate Health: (Includes the extensive Atrium Health network across the Carolinas).

Retail, Energy, and Automotive

Major corporations headquartered in North Carolina provide significant employment opportunities across diverse fields, from home improvement to energy infrastructure.

  • Lowe's: The Mooresville-based home improvement giant remains a favored employer.
  • Duke Energy: A critical infrastructure provider and major employer in the Charlotte region.
  • Alex Lee: The Hickory-based parent company of Lowes Foods and MDI (wholesale).
  • Sonic Automotive Inc.: One of the largest automotive retailers in the U.S., headquartered in Charlotte.
  • Belk: The long-standing department store chain continues to feature on the list.
