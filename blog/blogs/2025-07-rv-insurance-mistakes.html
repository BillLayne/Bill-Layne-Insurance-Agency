What's the biggest RV insurance mistake NC campers make?

The biggest RV insurance mistake NC campers make is assuming their auto insurance covers their RV. Standard auto insurance doesn't provide adequate coverage for RVs, leaving you exposed to massive liability and replacement cost gaps that can reach $50,000-$100,000 or more.

This is the number one mistake I see with families throughout the Yadkin Valley. Your auto insurance might provide some basic liability coverage, but it won't cover the full replacement cost of your RV or your personal belongings inside.

What Auto Insurance WON'T Cover for Your RV:

  • Full replacement cost for total loss situations
  • Personal property like camping gear and electronics
  • Emergency expenses for alternative lodging while traveling
  • Attached accessories like awnings and satellite dishes
  • Vacation liability while parked at campgrounds
  • Full-timer coverage if you live in your RV

Why do NC RV owners choose actual cash value instead of replacement cost?

NC RV owners often choose actual cash value (ACV) because it's cheaper initially, but this is a costly mistake. ACV coverage pays depreciated value while replacement cost pays full replacement price. A 5-year-old $50,000 RV might only receive $25,000-$30,000 under ACV coverage.

Here's the difference that can cost you tens of thousands:

Coverage Type What You Pay What You Get (Total Loss)
Replacement Cost $100-$200 more annually Full replacement value
Actual Cash Value Lower premium Depreciated value (50-70% less)

How much personal property coverage do I need for my RV in North Carolina?

Most NC RV owners need $15,000-$30,000 in personal property coverage, but many only carry $2,000-$5,000. Your camping gear, electronics, clothing, and equipment can easily total $20,000-$50,000, far exceeding standard coverage limits.

I always tell my clients to walk through their RV and add up everything inside. You'll be shocked at how quickly it adds up.

What emergency expense coverage do I need for RV travel in North Carolina?

Emergency expense coverage for RV travel in North Carolina should include $100-$150 per day for lodging, meals, and transportation if your RV breaks down or is in an accident. Without this coverage, a mechanical failure could cost you $1,000-$3,000 out-of-pocket for hotels and rental cars.

How often should I review my RV insurance coverage in North Carolina?

You should review your RV insurance coverage annually or whenever you make modifications, add equipment, or change how you use your RV. Many NC RV owners go years without updates, leaving themselves underinsured as their RV's value and equipment increase.

Here are the triggers that should prompt an immediate review:

When to Review Your RV Insurance:

  1. Added solar panels, generators, or other equipment
  2. Installed awnings, bike racks, or external accessories
  3. Changed from weekend camping to full-time living
  4. Started renting out your RV to others
  5. Moved to a new state or changed primary residence
  6. RV value increased due to market conditions

Annual Review Benefits
