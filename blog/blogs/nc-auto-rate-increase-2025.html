Why did my North Carolina car insurance go up in 2025?

Your North Carolina car insurance likely went up in 2025 due to a statewide average rate increase of 4.5% approved by the NC Department of Insurance. This increase is driven by rising costs for vehicle repairs, more expensive medical care after accidents, and an increase in the frequency and severity of claims across the state.

It's not just you, and it's not personal. Every insurance company in the state is facing the same economic pressures. Modern cars, with their advanced safety features like cameras and sensors in bumpers and windshields, are much more expensive to repair. A simple fender-bender that used to cost $500 can now easily exceed $2,500 due to the need for sensor recalibration. These costs are passed on to all drivers in the form of higher premiums.

What is the official 2025 auto insurance increase in North Carolina?

The official 2025 auto insurance increase for North Carolina is a statewide average of **4.5%**. This was a settlement reached between the NC Rate Bureau, which represents insurance companies, and the NC Department of Insurance. While the insurance industry initially requested a much larger 28.4% increase, the state negotiated it down significantly to protect consumers.

It's important to remember that 4.5% is just the *average*. Your individual rate change could be higher or lower depending on several factors:

  • Your Location: Drivers in more congested areas like Charlotte or Raleigh might see a slightly higher increase than those in rural areas like Surry County or the Yadkin Valley.
  • Your Driving Record: Any new tickets or accidents will cause your rate to increase far more than the statewide average.
  • Your Vehicle: If you drive a car that is particularly expensive to repair, your collision coverage premium may have increased more than average.
  • Your Insurance Carrier: Each company adjusts its rates differently based on its own claims experience.
