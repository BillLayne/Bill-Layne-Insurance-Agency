Why Your First Auto Insurance Check Might Seem Low 🤔

So, you've had an accident. You file a claim, and the insurance company sends you a check. But when you take it to the repair shop, their estimate is way higher. What gives?

What Is a Preliminary Estimate?

That first check is based on a "preliminary estimate." It's a quick assessment of the damage an adjuster can see on the outside. They look at the dents, scratches, and broken parts that are obvious. But modern cars are complex, and a lot of damage can be hidden under the hood or behind a bumper.

Common Gaps in Initial Estimates

  • Hidden Damage: Bent frames, cracked engine mounts, or damaged sensors that aren't visible at first glance.
  • OEM vs. Aftermarket Parts: The initial estimate might be based on cheaper aftermarket parts, but your repair shop might recommend Original Equipment Manufacturer (OEM) parts for a better fit and function.
  • Labor Rate Differences: The insurer's estimated labor rate might be lower than what your chosen high-quality repair shop charges.

Unpacking the NC Auto Insurance Claims Process 📋

Getting your car fixed right is a multi-step process. Here’s how it typically works in North Carolina.

1

File Your Claim

Contact your insurance agent (that's us!) or the company directly to report the accident. Provide as much detail as you can.

2

Get the Initial Check

An adjuster will assess the visible damage and the company will send you the first check based on that estimate. Remember, this is just the starting point!

3

Go to the Repair Shop

Take your car to a trusted repair shop. They will tear it down—meaning they'll take off the damaged parts to see what's underneath.

4

Request a Supplemental Check

If (and when) the shop finds more damage, they will contact your insurance company to file a "supplement." This is a request for additional funds. Once approved, the insurer sends a supplemental check to cover the extra costs.

Myths About Auto Insurance Checks busted! 💥

Myth: The first check is all you get.

Fact: This is completely false. The initial check is an estimate. The supplemental process exists specifically to cover hidden damages found by the professionals at the body shop.

Myth: Supplemental checks are complicated.

Fact: They don't have to be! Your repair shop and your insurance agent handle the paperwork. Your main job is to choose a good shop and stay in touch with your agent.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my auto insurance check not enough for car repairs in NC?

Your first check is based on visible damage only. A professional repair shop will often find hidden issues, which requires a second, "supplemental" check from the insurer to cover the full, actual cost of repairs.

How do I request a supplemental insurance check in North Carolina?

You don't have to! Your chosen repair shop will document the additional damage they find and submit a supplement request directly to the insurance company on your behalf.

Does accepting the first insurance check limit my claim?

No, it does not. Cashing the initial check does not mean your claim is closed. It is simply the first payment towards the total repair cost. You are still entitled to supplemental payments for any additional, approved damages.
