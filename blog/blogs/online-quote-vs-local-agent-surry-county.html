Why Does an Online Quote Cost the Same as a Local NC Agent?

Hey neighbor, this is the question we get all the time right here in Elkin NC: "Bill, why would I use you when I can just go to Progressive.com?" Great question — and the answer surprises most folks.

Here in North Carolina, every auto and home insurance carrier files its rates with the NC Department of Insurance. Those filed rates are what they're allowed to charge — period. Whether you click through Progressive's national TV ad, fill out a Nationwide form online, or walk into our office on N. Bridge Street, the same carrier with the same vehicle, same driver, and same coverage gives you the exact same price.

So where does the agent's commission come from? It's already baked into the carrier's filed rate — meaning whether or not an agent helps you, that commission is being paid out of every premium dollar. Skip the agent, and the carrier just keeps that piece. You don't save anything by going direct — you just lose the help.

Think of it like buying a house: a buyer's agent is paid out of the seller's commission whether you use one or not. Skipping the agent doesn't put money in your pocket — it just means you're navigating the deal alone.
