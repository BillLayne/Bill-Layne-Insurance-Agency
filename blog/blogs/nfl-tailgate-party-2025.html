Tailgate Like a Pro This NFL Season: Must-Know Auto Insurance Tips for NC Fans

The burgers are sizzling, the team colors are on, and the pre-game excitement is building. But is your insurance ready for the risks of the stadium parking lot?

By Bill Layne Insurance Team | Elkin, NC | Updated September 3, 2025

For football fans across North Carolina, tailgating is more than just a pre-game ritual—it’s an all-day event. From Charlotte to every corner of the state, fans gather with grills, coolers, and high spirits. But amid the fun, it's easy to overlook the unique risks that come with setting up camp in a crowded parking lot.

At Bill Layne Insurance, we want to ensure your game day is memorable for all the right reasons. Here’s what every NC fan needs to know about how their auto insurance—and other policies—come into play during a tailgate.

Responsible Hosting & Liability: It’s Not Just About the Food

If you're hosting the tailgate, you're not just in charge of the snacks; you also take on a degree of responsibility for your guests' safety. In North Carolina, while there isn't a strict "social host liability" law for serving alcohol to adults, you can still be found negligent if your actions (or inaction) contribute to an accident.

Even a simple accident at your setup, like someone tripping over a cooler and breaking an arm, could lead to a liability claim against you. Your homeowners or renters insurance policy includes personal liability coverage, which is designed to protect you in these situations. However, policies have limits, and a serious accident could easily exceed them.

Parking Lot Fender Benders & Mishaps

Stadium parking lots are notorious for tight spaces and distracted drivers. Even a low-speed bump can cause surprisingly expensive damage to modern vehicles.

Who Pays for What?

  • Collision Coverage: This part of your auto policy pays to repair your car if you hit another vehicle or an object (like a light pole), regardless of who is at fault. You will have to pay your deductible.
  • Property Damage Liability: If you are at fault for the accident, this coverage pays for the damage to the *other person's* car or property. This is mandatory in North Carolina.
  • Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UM/UIM): What if someone hits your parked car and drives off? This is where UM/UIM Property Damage coverage can be a lifesaver, helping to cover your repairs.

Protecting Your Gear: Is Your Grill Covered?

You've likely invested a lot in your tailgating setup: a portable grill, expensive cooler, team-branded chairs, maybe even a TV. What happens if these items are stolen from your truck bed or damaged by another vehicle?

"A common misconception is that your auto insurance covers personal items inside your vehicle. In reality, it only covers the vehicle itself."

Coverage for your personal belongings typically comes from your homeowners or renters insurance policy. This "personal property" coverage extends to your possessions even when they are away from your home, subject to your policy's deductible and any special limits.

The Risk: Grill Fire

A grease fire from your grill gets out of control and damages the paint on the car parked next to you. Your Property Damage Liability on your auto policy would likely respond to the car's damages, but the grill itself would fall under your homeowners/renters policy.

The Pro Move: Coverage Check

Before the season starts, review your homeowners or renters policy to understand your personal property limits and deductible. An inventory of your tailgate gear can be helpful if you ever need to file a claim.
