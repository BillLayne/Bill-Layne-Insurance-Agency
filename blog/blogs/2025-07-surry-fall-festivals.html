Your Ultimate 2025 Surry County Fall Bucket List

We believe in living life to the fullest, and that starts right here in our backyard. As your local insurance partners, we're not just protecting your assets; we're invested in your joy. Here’s our handpicked list of can't-miss fall experiences for 2025.

1. Embrace the Festival Spirit

  • Autumn Leaves Festival™ (October 10-12, 2025): The crown jewel of Mount Airy's fall season. It's more than just crafts and food; it's a community reunion. Go for the music, stay for the parade, and don't leave without a bag of fresh apple cider donuts.
  • Sonker Festival (October 4, 2025): Head to the Edwards-Franklin House in Lowgap for a true taste of Surry County heritage. This unique, deep-dish fruit dessert is a local treasure. Celebrate our history, one delicious bite at a time.
  • Yadkin Valley Pumpkin Festival (September 27, 2025): Elkin's Main Street comes alive! From the giant pumpkin weigh-off to the local vendors, this is small-town charm at its absolute best.

2. Get Lost in Farm Fun (The Good Kind!)

Before you head out, remember that farms are businesses with unique risks. A quick chat with us can ensure your family outing is protected from unexpected liabilities. It's the peace of mind you need to truly enjoy the day.

  • Miss Angels Farm & Orchard: Just outside Mount Airy, this is your one-stop-shop for apple picking, hayrides, and creating memories. Their corn maze is a rite of passage for local kids!
  • The Farm in Dobson: Known for their sprawling pumpkin patch and family-friendly activities. It's the perfect place to find that prize-winning pumpkin for your front porch.

3. Chase the Colors: Scenic Drives & Hikes

Surry County's beauty is unmatched in the fall. Make sure your auto policy is as solid as the granite on Pilot Mountain before you hit these scenic routes.

  • The Blue Ridge Parkway: An obvious choice for a reason. Enter near Fancy Gap and drive north or south for breathtaking vistas. Pro-tip: Go on a weekday to avoid the biggest crowds.
  • Pilot Mountain State Park: Hike to the top of the "Big Pinnacle" for a 360-degree view of the fall canopy. It’s a view that reminds you why we choose to live here.
  • The "Backroads Tour": Our personal favorite. Just pick a road like Zephyr Road or Rockford Road and drive. You'll discover hidden valleys and vibrant colors the tourists never see. This is the real Surry County.
