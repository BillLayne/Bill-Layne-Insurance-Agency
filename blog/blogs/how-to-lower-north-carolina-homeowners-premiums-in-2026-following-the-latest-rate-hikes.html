Frequently Asked Questions

Why did NC home insurance rates go up in 2026?
The primary drivers are inflation in building materials and increased reinsurance costs. The NC Rate Bureau adjusts rates based on the cost to repair homes and the frequency of severe weather events affecting the state.
What is Consent to Rate in North Carolina?
Consent to Rate (CTR) is a form that allows insurers to charge more than the maximum rate set by the state. If you sign this, your premium can be significantly higher (often 250% of the base rate). Always ask your agent if your policy includes a CTR surcharge.
Does credit score affect home insurance in NC?
Yes, North Carolina law allows insurers to use your insurance credit score to determine premiums. A higher credit score generally correlates with lower insurance rates, as statistical data suggests those with higher scores file fewer claims.
Is a $2,500 deductible worth it?
For most NC homeowners, yes. Raising your deductible from $1,000 to $2,500 can reduce your premium by 15-25%. The savings usually pay for the difference in deductible cost within 3-4 years of claim-free history.
Can I negotiate my home insurance rate?
You cannot negotiate the base rate, but you can adjust coverages. You can lower premiums by removing unnecessary floaters, increasing deductibles, or finding a carrier that places you in a more favorable "tier" based on your home's profile.
Do I need flood insurance in Elkin, NC?
Standard homeowners policies do NOT cover flood damage. Even in the foothills, flash flooding from heavy storms is a risk. If you are near the Yadkin River or a creek, we highly recommend a separate flood policy.
