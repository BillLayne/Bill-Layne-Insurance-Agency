What are the most important NC school safety tips?

The most important North Carolina school safety tips involve securing transportation, practicing digital citizenship, and protecting your home. This includes reviewing bus stop rules with your kids, setting clear guidelines for online activity and social media, and ensuring your home insurance covers valuable school electronics like laptops and tablets.

Safety is paramount. As you prepare for the new year, take time to cover these critical areas:

  • Transportation Safety: If your child walks, bike, or takes the bus, review the route with them. Remind them of traffic safety rules and stranger danger. For new teen drivers, this is a crucial time to set rules about passengers, curfews, and distracted driving.
  • Digital Safety: Discuss the importance of online privacy, responsible social media use, and identifying cyberbullying. Set up parental controls on devices as needed.
  • Property Protection: With expensive laptops, tablets, and musical instruments going to and from school, make sure your homeowners or renters insurance policy provides adequate coverage for personal belongings away from home.

Why should I review my insurance for back-to-school?

Reviewing your insurance for back-to-school is vital because your risks change. A new teen driver requires adding them to your auto policy, carpooling increases your liability, and expensive school-issued technology may need extra coverage under your home or renters insurance. A quick review ensures you're not left with costly gaps in coverage.

The back-to-school season is the perfect trigger for an annual insurance review. Here's what to look for:

  • Auto Insurance: Are you adding a teen driver to your policy? They need to be properly insured before they get behind the wheel. Also, ask about Good Student Discounts! If you're now part of a carpool, you might want to review your liability limits.
  • Homeowners/Renters Insurance: Does your policy cover that new $1,500 laptop the school requires? Check your personal property limits and consider a special endorsement for high-value items. This also applies to college students living in a dorm—their belongings might not be fully covered by your policy.

Don't wait for an incident to find out you're underinsured. A quick chat with your agent can provide peace of mind for the whole school year.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How can I get a Good Student Discount on my NC auto insurance?

Most insurance carriers in North Carolina offer a Good Student Discount for high school or full-time college students who maintain a certain GPA (usually a "B" average or 3.0). To get it, you'll typically need to provide a recent report card or transcript to your insurance agent as proof.

Is my child's school laptop covered by my homeowners insurance?

Generally, yes. Personal belongings, including electronics, are typically covered under the personal property portion of a standard homeowners or renters insurance policy, even when taken outside the home. However, coverage may be subject to your deductible, and high-value items might have coverage limits. It's best to confirm the specifics with your agent.

What liability concerns are there with carpooling?

When you carpool, you take on greater responsibility for the safety of the children in your vehicle. If an accident occurs and you are at fault, you could be held liable for their injuries. We recommend reviewing your policy's liability limits to ensure they are high enough to protect your assets in a worst-case scenario.
