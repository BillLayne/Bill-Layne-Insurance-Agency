Gap #1: The Floodwater Confusion

This is arguably the most dangerous gap for homeowners in North Carolina. Many people assume their standard policy covers all forms of water damage, but that's simply not true. Your policy covers "sudden and accidental" water damage, like a pipe bursting. It does NOT cover flooding, which is defined as water coming from the ground and entering your home.

Understanding the Difference: Water Damage vs. Flood Damage

Imagine your toilet overflows and soaks the bathroom floor. Your homeowners policy should cover this. Now, imagine a severe storm causes a nearby creek to overflow, and floodwaters enter your basement. This is considered flooding and is a massive, excluded loss.

Why You Need a Separate Flood Policy in North Carolina

Even if you don't live in a high-risk flood zone, North Carolina's weather can be unpredictable, from hurricanes to flash floods. A separate flood insurance policy from the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) is the only way to cover this risk. We can help you determine your risk and get the right policy for your home.

Gap #2: Your Valuables Are Not Fully Protected

Your standard homeowners policy has limits on expensive items. That new jewelry, antique furniture, or art collection? It's likely only covered up to a few thousand dollars, a fraction of its true value.

Low Limits on Jewelry, Art, and Electronics

While your policy provides broad coverage for your personal belongings, it will have sub-limits for certain categories. Common examples include:

  • Jewelry, watches, and furs (often limited to $1,500)
  • Firearms (often limited to $2,500)
  • Silverware and collectibles (often limited to $2,500)

Scheduling Personal Property for True Protection

To fill this gap, we can "schedule" or "endorse" these items onto your policy. This means listing each item with its specific value and ensuring it's covered for its full appraised amount, often with no deductible. It's a simple, affordable fix that provides significant peace of mind.

Gap #3: The Unpleasant Reality of Sewer & Drain Backups

Imagine coming home to a basement filled with sewage after a heavy rainstorm. It’s an expensive, devastating, and disgusting problem that most people assume is covered by their insurance. Unfortunately, it's not.

Why This Common Problem Is Often Excluded

Standard policies exclude sewer and drain backups because they are seen as a maintenance issue, not a "sudden and accidental" event. The damage, however, can run into tens of thousands of dollars.

The Solution: Adding a Water Backup Endorsement

This is a simple and inexpensive add-on to your policy that fills this critical gap. For as little as $50 a year, you can get $10,000-$25,000 in coverage for this common and destructive problem. It’s a no-brainer for any homeowner.

Gap #4: The Unseen Costs of a Home-Based Business

With more and more people working from home, this is a rapidly growing coverage gap. Your personal homeowners policy is not designed to cover business-related risks.

Personal vs. Business Property Coverage

If you have business equipment in your home—computers, printers, inventory—it's likely not covered under your standard policy. These items are considered business property and have very low coverage limits, if any. This is also true for detached structures like a shed or detached garage used for your business.

Liability Risks for Your Home-Based Business

If a client or delivery person gets injured on your property, your homeowners' liability coverage may not apply if the injury is related to your business. This could leave you vulnerable to a lawsuit.

Important: Your homeowners' policy will not cover business inventory, equipment, or liability. A separate business policy or a home-based business endorsement is essential to fill this gap and ensure you are fully protected.

Gap #5: Under-Insuring Your Home's Replacement Cost

In a volatile real estate market, homeowners often confuse their home's market value with its replacement cost. They are two very different numbers, and mixing them up can leave you with a massive coverage shortfall.

The Difference Between Market Value and Replacement Cost

Market value is what a buyer would pay for your home and land. Replacement cost is the amount it would take to completely rebuild your home from the ground up in the event of a total loss. This includes the cost of labor, materials, debris removal, and rebuilding at current prices. The latter is what your policy should be based on.

How Inflation Can Create a Huge Coverage Gap

Construction costs have soared in recent years. If you bought your home in Elkin five years ago, its current replacement cost is likely 30-40% higher than your original policy limits. That's why we automatically review our clients' replacement costs every year to ensure they are never underinsured.

Does a standard homeowners policy in NC cover mold or pest damage? +

No, a standard policy generally does not cover damage from mold or pests. These are typically considered maintenance issues. Some policies may offer a limited endorsement for mold, but it's often tied to a covered loss (e.g., mold from a burst pipe). It's crucial to address these issues promptly as a homeowner to prevent costly damage.

Can I add coverage for a home office to my existing policy? +

Yes, in most cases, you can add an endorsement to your homeowners policy to cover business property and liability for a home-based business. However, if your business is significant or has clients visiting the property, a separate business policy is often a better and more comprehensive solution. We can help you determine which option is best for your specific needs.

How often should I review my home's replacement cost? +

You should review your policy's replacement cost at least once a year, and definitely after any major renovations or upgrades. Given the fluctuations in construction costs and inflation, an annual review is the best way to ensure your coverage keeps pace with the cost to rebuild your home.
