Understanding ACV (Actual Cash Value)

Insurance companies often settle claims based on Actual Cash Value (ACV) — which is the replacement cost minus depreciation for the item's age and condition.

Example:

A 3-year-old TV that costs $800 new today with a 7-year useful life:

ACV = $800 × (1 − 3/7) = $800 × 0.571 = $457.14

If you have Replacement Cost coverage, your policy may pay the full $800 after you replace the item. This tool shows both values.
