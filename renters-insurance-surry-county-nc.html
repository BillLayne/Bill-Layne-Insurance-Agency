What Does Renters Insurance Cover?

Renters insurance (also called an HO-4 policy) protects three key areas that your landlord's policy does not.

Personal Property

Covers your furniture, electronics, clothing, appliances, and other belongings if they are damaged or stolen. Typical coverage: $20,000-$50,000.

Personal Liability

Protects you if someone is injured in your rental unit and sues you. Also covers accidental damage you cause to the rental property. Typical coverage: $100,000-$300,000.

Additional Living Expenses

Pays for temporary housing, meals, and other costs if your rental becomes uninhabitable due to a covered loss like a fire or major water leak.

Why Surry County Renters Need Coverage

Your Landlord's Insurance Does NOT Cover Your Belongings

Your landlord's dwelling fire or property policy covers the building structure only — the walls, roof, and foundation. If a fire, break-in, or burst pipe damages your personal property, you are on your own without renters insurance.

🌪️

Ice Storms & Power Outages

Surry County ice storms can knock out power for days. Renters insurance covers spoiled food and can pay for temporary housing if pipes freeze and burst in your apartment.

🔓

Theft & Break-Ins

If your apartment or rental home is burglarized, renters insurance reimburses you for stolen electronics, jewelry, and other personal items up to your policy limit.

💧

Water Damage from Above

If the tenant above you has a burst pipe or overflowing tub that damages your belongings, your renters insurance covers the loss — you don't have to chase the other tenant.

⚠️

Liability Protection

If a guest slips and falls in your rental, or your dog bites a neighbor, renters insurance covers legal fees and medical bills — potentially saving you tens of thousands of dollars.

What Renters Insurance Does NOT Cover

Understanding exclusions helps you avoid surprises at claim time.

✗

Flood Damage

Requires a separate flood insurance policy. Important for rentals near the Yadkin or Mitchell rivers.

✗

Earthquake Damage

Not covered by standard renters policies. Earthquake endorsements may be available in NC.

✗

Your Roommate's Belongings

Your policy only covers you. Roommates need their own renters insurance policies.

✗

Items in Your Car

Belongings stolen from your vehicle are covered by auto comprehensive, not renters insurance.

✗

High-Value Items Over Sub-Limits

Jewelry, firearms, and collectibles have sub-limits (typically $1,500). You may need a scheduled personal property endorsement for full coverage.

✗

Pest & Rodent Damage

Damage caused by bed bugs, termites, mice, or other pests is considered a maintenance issue and is not covered.

Renters Insurance FAQs

How much does renters insurance cost?

$15-$30/month in Surry County NC. Exact cost depends on coverage amount, deductible, and location. Bundling with auto saves 5-15%.

Is renters insurance required in NC?

Not by law, but many landlords require it in your lease. Even without a requirement, it is strongly recommended since your landlord's policy does NOT cover your belongings.

Does my landlord's policy cover me?

No. Your landlord's insurance covers the building only. Your furniture, electronics, clothing, and other personal property are not covered without your own renters policy.

Does it cover my roommate?

No. Each renter needs their own policy. Spouses or domestic partners may be added, but roommates need separate coverage.

Does it cover flooding?

No. Flood damage requires a separate flood insurance policy. This is especially important for rentals near Surry County rivers.

What about expensive jewelry or guns?

Standard policies have sub-limits (usually $1,500 for jewelry, $2,500 for firearms). You can add a scheduled personal property endorsement for full coverage on high-value items.
