Frequently Asked Questions

When is REAL ID required in North Carolina?

REAL ID is now required for domestic commercial flights and to enter federal facilities. If you haven't upgraded yet, schedule an appointment at your local DMV office.

Can I renew my tags if I have unpaid property taxes?

No. North Carolina uses the Tag & Tax Together system, meaning you must pay your vehicle property taxes at the same time as your registration renewal. They cannot be separated.

Is there a grace period for expired NC tags?

Legally, there is no grace period for driving with expired tags. However, the DMV allows a 15-day window before late fees are applied to the renewal transaction itself.

How do I avoid the $50 insurance lapse fine?

Always turn in your license plate to the DMV BEFORE you cancel your auto insurance. Canceling insurance first triggers an automatic notification and fine from the DMV.

What is the "FS5" number?

The FS5 is a unique control number provided by your insurer or on your DMV notice. You need it to clear an insurance lapse fine and restore your registration.

What are the new NC minimum liability limits?

As of July 2025, North Carolina requires $50,000 per person / $100,000 per accident bodily injury and $50,000 property damage (50/100/50). Contact your agent to verify your policy meets these limits.

Are emissions tests required in every NC county?

No. Safety inspections are required statewide, but emissions testing is only required in 19 specific NC counties. Check the NCDMV website for the full list.

Can I renew my license online if it's already expired?

If expired less than two years, you can often renew online if you meet eligibility requirements. If expired over two years, you must visit a DMV office in person.

How long do I have to update my address with the DMV?

North Carolina law requires you to notify the DMV of a change of address within 60 days of your move. This can be done free online in most cases.
