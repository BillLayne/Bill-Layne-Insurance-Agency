Common Questions

Standard policies exclude floods, earthquakes, maintenance issues, and pest infestations. Flood insurance requires a separate policy.
You need enough to REBUILD your home at current labor and material costs, which is often different from your home's market value.
Replacement Cost pays for a new item. Actual Cash Value (ACV) pays for the item minus depreciation (what it's worth used). We recommend Replacement Cost.
No. Standard homeowners insurance does not cover flood damage. You need a separate flood insurance policy, typically through the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) or a private flood insurer. Even if you're not in a designated flood zone, over 25% of flood claims come from moderate-to-low risk areas. We can help you get a flood quote.
Roof age is one of the biggest rating factors. A newer roof (under 10 years) can qualify for significant discounts. Roofs over 20 years may face higher premiums or limited coverage. Some carriers switch from replacement cost to actual cash value (ACV) for older roofs. If you've recently replaced your roof, make sure your agent knows — it could lower your premium.
Many NC homeowners policies have a separate wind/hail deductible, often 1-2% of your dwelling coverage. This means if your home is insured for $300,000, your wind/hail deductible could be $3,000-$6,000 — much higher than your standard deductible. Given NC's exposure to hurricanes and severe storms, understanding this deductible is critical.
In most cases, yes. Bundling home and auto with the same carrier can save you up to 25% on your premiums. However, as an independent agency, we sometimes find that splitting policies between two carriers actually saves more. We'll compare both options and show you which approach gives you the best rate.
Good news — a tree falling on your home is typically covered under standard homeowners insurance, whether it's your tree or a neighbor's. Your policy covers the structural damage and usually includes some coverage for debris removal. However, a tree that falls in your yard but doesn't hit anything is generally not covered. Preventive tree removal is also not covered.
